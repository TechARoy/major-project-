Grand Canyon

Introduction

The Grand Canyon is a vast and iconic natural wonder located in the state of Arizona, USA. Carved by the Colorado River, it is known for its immense size, breathtaking views, and layered bands of red rock revealing millions of years of geological history.

History

The Grand Canyon has been inhabited by Native American tribes for thousands of years. It became a national monument in 1908 and was later designated a national park in 1919. It is one of the most visited national parks in the United States.

Geography and Features

The Grand Canyon stretches 277 miles (446 km) in length, up to 18 miles (29 km) wide, and over a mile (1.6 km) deep. It features steep canyon walls, the flowing Colorado River, and unique rock formations that attract geologists and tourists alike.

Tourism

Millions of visitors from around the world travel to see the Grand Canyon each year. Popular activities include hiking, rafting, helicopter tours, and visiting observation points such as the South Rim, North Rim, and the Skywalk.
