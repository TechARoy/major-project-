Buckingham Palace

Introduction

Buckingham Palace is the London residence and administrative headquarters of the monarch of the United Kingdom. It is one of the most iconic buildings in Britain and serves as the center of state occasions and royal hospitality.

History

Originally known as Buckingham House, it was built in 1703 for the Duke of Buckingham. The building was acquired by King George III in 1761 as a private residence for Queen Charlotte and became the official royal palace of the British monarch on the accession of Queen Victoria in 1837.

Architecture and Features

The palace features 775 rooms, including 19 state rooms, 52 royal and guest bedrooms, 188 staff bedrooms, 92 offices, and 78 bathrooms. Its famous balcony is often used for royal appearances and celebrations.

Tourism

Buckingham Palace is a major tourist attraction, especially during the summer months when parts of the palace are open to the public. The Changing of the Guard ceremony is one of its most well-known events.
