Big Ben

Introduction

Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell of the striking clock at the north end of the Palace of Westminster in London. It is often extended to refer to both the clock and the clock tower.

History

Completed in 1859, Big Ben has become one of the most prominent symbols of the United Kingdom. The clock and its tower were designed by Augustus Pugin in a neo-Gothic style during the Victorian era.

Architecture and Features

The Elizabeth Tower stands at 316 feet (96 meters) tall and houses four clock faces, each 23 feet (7 meters) in diameter. The Great Bell, known as Big Ben, weighs over 13 tons and chimes every hour.

Tourism

Big Ben attracts millions of tourists annually. Although public tours are currently limited to UK residents, the tower remains a highlight of any visit to London, especially when lit up at night.
